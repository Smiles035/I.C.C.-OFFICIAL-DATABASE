DESIGNATION: The Swarm

SCIENTIFIC CLASSIFICATION:

Parasitic Migratory Xenoform Collective

THREAT LEVEL:

SEVERE

--------------------------------------------------

SUMMARY:

The Swarm is an extraterrestrial parasitic

lifeform observed migrating between planetary

systems through unknown means.


The organism is responsible for multiple

documented biosphere collapse events across

charted space.


Unlike conventional apex organisms, the

Swarm does not engage in direct predatory

behavior on a macroscopic scale.


Instead, the Swarm spreads through microscopic

parasitic infestation of host organisms,

resulting in long-term ecological destabilization,

neurological degradation, and total biological

collapse of infected environments.

--------------------------------------------------

PRIMARY ORGANISM:


Adult Swarm organisms resemble insectoid

aerial parasites possessing:

- elongated body structures

- thin multi-jointed limbs

- reinforced piercing appendages

- membranous atmospheric wings


Average adult length:

14-22 centimeters


Primary biological function:

implantation of parasitic egg clusters

into living hosts.


Adult organisms demonstrate:

- collective migratory behavior

- attraction to high biomass environments

- resistance to conventional pesticides

- rapid adaptation to atmospheric conditions

--------------------------------------------------

PARASITIC STAGE:


Following host penetration, microscopic

egg structures are introduced directly

into the bloodstream.


Eggs hatch into multicellular worm-like

parasites approximately equal in size

to biological blood cells.


These organisms are capable of:

- unrestricted circulatory movement

- tissue infiltration

- rapid internal reproduction

- neurological penetration

- organ colonization


Due to microscopic scale, complete removal

of infestation is currently impossible using

standard medical procedures.

--------------------------------------------------

INFESTATION PROGRESSION

STAGE I — INITIAL EXPOSURE

Symptoms include:

- fatigue

- elevated temperature

- headaches

- respiratory irritation

- auditory abnormalities


Subjects remain externally stable.

--------------------------------------------------

STAGE II — SYSTEMIC COLONIZATION

Symptoms include:

- internal hemorrhaging

- vascular discoloration

- cognitive instability

- hallucinations

- emotional irregularities

- sleep disruption


Parasite population expands exponentially.

--------------------------------------------------

STAGE III — ADVANCED INFESTATION

Symptoms include:

- progressive organ failure

- severe neurological degradation

- tissue deformation

- motor dysfunction

- behavioral collapse


Subjects may exhibit:

- compulsive migration behavior

- fixation toward infected zones

- unresponsiveness to pain


--------------------------------------------------


STAGE IV — TERMINAL COLLAPSE

Host biological systems fail completely.


Large-scale parasitic emergence may occur

following host death.


Environmental contamination risk:

EXTREME


--------------------------------------------------

PSYCHOLOGICAL EFFECTS:


Heavily infected individuals frequently report:

- shared dreams

- whispered vocalizations

- visual movement in peripheral vision

- sensations of observation

- compulsive thoughts


Research suggests Swarm organisms may

communicate through biochemical or

neurological signaling mechanisms.


Collective intelligence status:

UNCONFIRMED

--------------------------------------------------

PLANETARY EFFECTS:


Confirmed Swarm infestation events result in:

- ecosystem destabilization

- atmospheric contamination

- mass extinction events

- biomass corruption

- irreversible environmental collapse


Average planetary survival duration after

major infestation:

4-11 years


--------------------------------------------------

KRYION RESEARCH STATUS:


HB-7 samples extracted from Kryion demonstrate

partial inhibitory effects against parasitic

reproduction and tissue colonization.


Complete neutralization has not been achieved.


Research priority elevated under direct order

of Administrator Elias N. Varn.


--------------------------------------------------

ADMINISTRATOR MEDICAL CONNECTION:


Administrator Varn is currently diagnosed with

Xenocellular Carcinoma (X.C.C.), believed to

originate from prolonged exposure to dormant

Swarm organisms during early deep-space travel.


Subject survival remains dependent on:

- experimental HB-7 procedures

- synthetic organ replacement

- neurological preservation systems

- O.V.I.S. assisted stabilization


--------------------------------------------------

INTERNAL NOTICE:


Public awareness of the Swarm remains prohibited

under I.C.C. Security Directive 47-A.

Unauthorized disclosure of infestation data

may result in:

- immediate detainment

- memory suppression procedures

- termination authorization


--------------------------------------------------

A CONVENTIONAL SPECIES.


INFESTATION CAN REMAIN UNDETECTED

FOR EXTENDED PERIODS.


ALL PERSONNEL ARE INSTRUCTED TO REPORT:

- unexplained illness

- auditory abnormalities

- blood discoloration

- unusual behavioral changes
